How Noise Shapes Psychological Stress in New York City

This project explores whether neighborhoods with higher noise complaints also report higher psychological stress.

Hypothesis: Neighborhoods with higher noise complaints have higher psychological stress levels.

Feel the Noise

Hover over each area to see how intense the noise exposure is. Stronger, faster ripples represent heavier noise burdens.

Noise is Never Distributed Evenly

Some neighborhoods carry much heavier sound burdens because of nightlife, traffic corridors, or density.

Noise vs Stress: Do They Move Together?

This scatter plot compares neighborhood noise complaints with reported stress levels. Points along the diagonal show the expected pattern: higher noise and higher stress. Outliers highlight places where stress is shaped by more than sound alone.

Conclusion

Noise helps explain part of the psychological burden in New York, but not all of it. Some neighborhoods with heavy noise do not report equally high stress, suggesting that social and economic factors also shape how stress is felt.
